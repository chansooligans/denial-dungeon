# Aesthetic & Mood Inspirations

Reference list for visual and audio design of The Waiting Room and overall game feel.

## Film / Visual
- **Terry Gilliam's Brazil (1985)** — Bureaucratic surrealism. Endless forms, pneumatic tubes, impossible architecture. The absurdity of systems designed by nobody and understood by nobody. Key reference for The Waiting Room's visual language.
- **Spirited Away (2001)** — The bathhouse as a surreal workplace. Warm but overwhelming. Characters who are strange but sympathetic. The feeling of being a newcomer in a world with rules you don't understand yet.
- **David Lynch / Twin Peaks** — The uncanny familiar. Ordinary spaces (diners, offices, waiting rooms) made deeply strange through lighting, pacing, and sound design. The Red Room as a reference for The Waiting Room's "between" quality.

## Games
- **Animal Crossing** — Cozy overworld, NPC relationships, slice-of-life warmth. Reference for the normal hospital layer.
- **Pokemon** — Turn-based encounters, type effectiveness, collection/codex. Reference for the battle system and denial codex.
- **Celeste** — Tight, responsive controls. Serious themes treated with care. Reference for how to handle heavy subject matter without being heavy-handed.
- **Hades** — Between-run progression, NPC storytelling, procedural variety. Reference for how the game gets richer over time.

## Audio Direction (for later)
- Normal hospital: warm ambient (AC hum, distant PA announcements, keyboard clicks, coffee machine)
- The Waiting Room: unsettling ambient (endlessly ringing phone, ticking clock that skips beats, distant stamp impacts, paper shuffling, muzak played slightly too slow)
- Transitions: a cracking/shifting sound when moving between layers
- Battle music: low tension, procedural — builds with encounter difficulty
