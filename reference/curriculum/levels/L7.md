# L7 · The Courtroom — "What did the payer decide?"

The remittance arrives. Time to read the verdict. The level
where the player learns to read an 835 ERA as the legal
document it is.

## Concepts

- Adjudication.
- The 835 ERA — what each segment means.
- CARC vs RARC.
- Allowed amount, paid amount, contractual adjustment,
  patient responsibility.
- **Underpayments — paid less than contract.** (Gap: huge in
  real RCM. AR analysts spend half their time here.)
- Partial payments.
- Remit posting.
- **AR aging buckets — 0–30 / 31–60 / 61–90 / 90+.** (Gap:
  triage logic.)

## Documents

- 835 ERA.
- EOB.
- Payer letter.
- **AR aging report.** (Gap.)
- **Contract-vs-paid variance report.** (Gap.)

## Actors

- Payer claim adjudicator (offstage; voice quoted in payer
  letters).
- Payment poster.
- AR analyst.
- **Underpayment specialist.** (Gap NPC.)

## Hospital rooms

- Payment posting area.
- AR floor (bullpen of analysts working aging buckets).
- Cash room (where the money actually lands).
- **Underpayment desk.** (Gap.)

## Waiting Room stations

- Courtroom of stamped denials (general atmosphere).
- **Timely Filing Reaper** ✅ existing (`co_29_reaper`).
- **Underpayment Specter** *(gap)* — appears to pay in full,
  then short-changes you on the way out. Cosmological
  resonance: every contract underpaid by the payer.
- **Aging-Bucket Layers** *(gap)* — older claims sink to
  deeper, dustier rooms. The 90+ floor is dim and quiet.

## Gaps

- Underpayment Specter encounter + matching case (a claim
  that paid 60% of contract; player has to spot it and
  recover).
- Aging-bucket treatment — visual layers in the Waiting
  Room AR floor where deeper = older, with surreal physics
  (the 90+ room has a different gravity).
- Underpayment specialist NPC.
- An ERA-reading lesson — the player gets handed a real 835
  and has to interpret it.

## Cross-cutting concerns introduced

- **Denial taxonomy (CARC/RARC)** (formal introduction here;
  player has been seeing CARCs since L1 but the system view
  comes here).
- **Time pressure (filing windows)** (Reaper makes it
  literal).
- **AR aging / triage** (touches L8 patient billing).

## Narrative beats

Dana's L7 reveal beat is **the glimpse**:

- The player sees Dana in the Waiting Room — briefly,
  distantly. A figure at a desk that vanishes when looked at
  directly.
- When asked the next day, Dana doesn't remember.
- The player *knows* now. Doesn't have the vocabulary for it
  yet, but knows.
- Page 9 (drafted) introduces the marginal annotation
  device — a smaller, adjacent voice writes:
  *"Sometimes you write it off because the patient died.
  Don't forget that's a verdict too."*
