# L10 · The Audit — "Was any of this defensible?"

Everything the player did is under review. The level where
every shortcut from earlier levels comes back. Also the level
where the door home opens for Dana — see
[`../../narrative/endings.md`](../../narrative/endings.md)
(provisional).

## Concepts

- RAC (Recovery Audit Contractors).
- Medicare integrity contractors.
- Payer pre-payment audits.
- Documentation defense.
- Coding compliance.
- Upcoding / downcoding.
- The False Claims Act (background; don't go deep — just
  enough that the player understands the stakes).
- Medical record (M&R) requests.
- Recoupments (the payer takes back money already paid).

## Documents

- Audit demand letter.
- Requested chart packet (the player has to assemble this).
- Response template.
- Recoupment letter.

## Actors

- Auditor (the boss; cool, polite, devastating).
- Compliance officer.
- You defending your earlier work.
- The medical director who signs off on the final defense.

## Hospital rooms

- Conference room (where the auditors arrive).
- Around-the-table workspace where the audit packet gets
  built.

## Waiting Room stations

- Mirror room — every shortcut from earlier levels reflected
  back.
- **The Quarterly Audit** ✅ existing boss (`boss_audit`).
- **Dana's last claim file** — surfaces here per the
  narrative arc; defending it == pulling Dana back. See
  [`../../narrative/endings.md`](../../narrative/endings.md).

## Existing content

- `boss_audit`, `case_audit_finale`.

## Gaps

- The "audit packet assembly" scene — the player gathers
  documentation for every cited claim. Mechanically novel;
  this isn't combat, this is *defense*.
- The medical director NPC (signs off on the packet).
- The auditor as a *character* with a voice. (Today the
  audit is mechanical; for L10 to land, the auditor should
  feel like a person who is doing their job correctly *and*
  unsympathetically.)

## Cross-cutting concerns paid off here

- **Compliance / documentation defense** (this is the level
  it was built for; every L4-L6 shortcut shows up).
- **Denial taxonomy** (the auditor cites every CARC the
  player didn't fight properly).
- **Patient experience** (in the audit packet — patient
  outcomes are part of the defense).

## Narrative beats

Dana's L10 reveal beat is **the audit itself**:

- The audit isn't just an audit — it's the door home.
- A claim defended with full documentation can pull a trapped
  soul back to the surface.
- Dana's last claim is in the file.
- Defending it alongside the player's own work pulls her
  back. Mechanically: same UI as defending any other case in
  the audit; thematically: a claim resolved is a soul
  released.
- Page 12 (drafted as flexible) is delivered here. See
  [`../../narrative/notebook/pages.md`](../../narrative/notebook/pages.md).
