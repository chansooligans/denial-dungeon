# L4 · The Copy — "Does the chart say what the bill says?"

Clinical documentation meets billing codes. The level where
documentation literacy becomes a real skill — and the level
where the player first notices something is off about Dana
(see
[`../../narrative/notebook/reveal-timeline.md`](../../narrative/notebook/reveal-timeline.md)).

This is the **worked-example level** — see
[`../worked-examples/L4.md`](../worked-examples/L4.md) for the
full hospital-room ↔ Waiting Room station mapping.

## Concepts

- Clinical documentation (the chart as truth).
- CDI queries — what they are, how to write them so they
  aren't leading.
- Specificity (I50.9 vs I50.42).
- Severity coding (CCs / MCCs).
- Diagnosis vs reason for visit.
- Principal vs secondary diagnoses.
- **Introduction to charge capture / CDM.** (Gap: charge
  capture starts here as "what got documented but didn't
  appear on the bill?" Continues into L6 for the deep
  treatment.)

## Narrative beats

L4 is the level where Dana shows the **first cracks**:

- She misses a meeting she should be at; comes in late, says
  she "got lost downstairs." Smiles it off.
- A page in the notebook now ends mid-sentence (page 6 —
  drafted in
  [`../../narrative/notebook/pages.md`](../../narrative/notebook/pages.md)).
- A coffee cup with no coffee in it.

The metaphor foreshadows itself — the level where the player
learns about *unfinished documentation* is the level where
they get the first hint Dana herself is unfinished
documentation.
