# L5 · The Library — "What do the rules actually say?"

Payer policies are public; most denials are predictable. The
level where the player learns that the rules of the system
aren't secret — they're just boring.

**This level is mostly a gap.** It's the most underbuilt level
relative to its real-world weight. Building it from scratch is
recommended over retrofitting.

## Concepts

- Medical policy (LCD / NCD).
- NCCI edits.
- Payer policy bulletins.
- Medical necessity definitions.
- **Fee schedules and contract terms.** (Gap: actually teach
  contract math — allowed amount, lesser-of, withholds,
  carve-outs.)
- **Vendor: policy research databases** (offstage).

## Documents

- LCD article.
- NCCI edit table.
- Payer policy PDF.
- **Contract excerpt with fee schedule attachment.** (Gap.)
- Payer provider manual.

## Actors

- Payer medical director (offstage).
- **Contract manager.** (Gap NPC — needs authoring; see
  [`../open-questions.md`](../open-questions.md), question 2.)
- You as researcher.

## Hospital rooms

- Contract management office.
- A literal library of binders that quietly updates itself.
  (Plays well in the cozy register.)

## Waiting Room stations

- Infinite shelves whose pages reorder when you look away.
- **Fee Schedule Cartographer** *(gap archetype)* — a figure
  who keeps redrawing the math on a giant sheet that never
  finishes. Cosmological resonance: every contract math
  nobody reread.
- Possibly a librarian-spirit who asks for the right
  citation before letting you proceed.

## Gaps (most of the level)

- A contract-math encounter — the player has to figure out if
  the payer paid correctly per contract.
- Fee Schedule Cartographer archetype.
- Contract manager NPC + dialogue.
- Medical director NPC (offstage; possibly only seen in
  payer letters quoted on the page).
- An LCD-reading scene — Dana's page 7 says "the rules the
  system follows aren't a secret — they're just boring.
  Boring is a kind of hiding." Make the player read one.

## Cross-cutting concerns introduced

- **Contract terms / fee schedules** (touches L7 underpayments
  via the variance report).
- **Vendor management** (policy research databases — paid off
  fully at L6).

## Narrative beats

Dana's L5 reveal beat is **ambient**:

- She sometimes refers to a meeting that hasn't happened yet.
  Doesn't seem to notice the slip.
- The handwriting on a recent page doesn't quite match her
  hand. Closer than a forgery; further than a draft.
- She gives the player a *blank* notebook around this level
  ("I think you're ready" — apprentice → peer transition;
  see [`../../narrative/notebook/README.md`](../../narrative/notebook/README.md)).

## Open questions

- Is the Library a *physical* library room, or is it more a
  metaphysical concept the player navigates by document type?
- The Fee Schedule Cartographer — funny enough?
- L5 is the level where the player feels like they've been
  given keys to the system. How do we make that satisfying
  without it being a lecture?
