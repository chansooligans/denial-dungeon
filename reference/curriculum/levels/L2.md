# L2 · The Front Door — "Did the front desk get it right?"

Registration is where claims are born — and where they first
break.

## Concepts

- Patient access; demographics.
- Insurance verification (270/271 transactions).
- Benefit basics.
- **Plan types** — HMO / PPO / EPO / HDHP / HMO-MA / PPO-MA /
  Medicaid managed care. (Gap: payer-type literacy.)
- In-network vs out-of-network.
- **Eligibility-vendor workflow.** (Gap: vendor management —
  eligibility APIs aren't always the payer.)
- COB chain initialization.

## Documents

- Registration form.
- Insurance card.
- 271 response.
- Benefits summary.

## Actors

- Registrar.
- Financial counselor.
- **The patient at the front desk** (per design principle —
  patients can speak in Hospital rooms).
- Vendor's eligibility platform (offstage; the player doesn't
  meet a vendor rep until L6).

## Hospital rooms

- Registration desk.
- Self-serve kiosks.
- Financial counseling office.
- Manager's office.

## Waiting Room stations

- **Eligibility Fog** ✅ existing (`eligibility_fog`).
- **Wrong Card mirror** *(gap)* — the patient handed over a
  card that no longer exists; the spirit hands you back
  unreadable IDs. Cosmological resonance: every claim sent to
  a stale plan.

## Gaps

- Payer-type literacy encounter (a case where the wrong plan
  type was selected at registration → wrong network → denial).
- Wrong Card archetype.
- Patient-facing dialogue at the registration desk — what's
  it like to be the patient who hands over the wrong card?
- Eligibility vendor as an actor (probably teased here and
  paid off in L6).

## Cross-cutting concerns introduced

- **COB initialization** (full COB doesn't fight until L9).
- **Vendor management** (full treatment at L6).
- **Patient experience** (a face at the front desk; weight
  carries to L8).
