# L9 · The Maze — "Whose plan pays first?"

Coordination of benefits. Multiple payers, one claim. The
level where the player learns the contract chain is *contractual*,
not optional.

## Concepts

- COB chain.
- Primary / secondary / tertiary payers.
- Medicare Secondary Payer rules.
- Birthday rule for kids of divorced parents.
- TRICARE / VA edge cases.
- OA-23 cascading (the adjustment that triggers the next
  payer).
- "The spouse's plan" trap.

## Documents

- Primary 835.
- Secondary claim.
- COB questionnaire.

## Actors

- Member services (payer side).
- COB desk (hospital side).
- The patient (again — they're often the one who has to fix
  the COB chain at the payer level).

## Hospital rooms

- COB desk.
- Spreadsheet hell (literal — a corner of the office where
  the COB analyst's screens are full of Excel).

## Waiting Room stations

- **Coordination Hydra** ✅ existing (`oa_23_hydra`). Three
  heads, three payers; you adjudicate them in order.

## Existing content

- `oa_23_hydra`, `case_hydra_okwu`.

## Gaps

- A birthday-rule scenario (which parent's plan is primary
  for a child).
- A Medicare-secondary scenario (Medicare paying second to a
  commercial plan, which trips up half the industry).
- A "spouse's plan" trap (the patient said spouse, the
  spouse said the patient).

## Cross-cutting concerns introduced

- **COB** as a system (introduced at L2 in passing; full
  treatment here).

## Narrative beats

Dana's L9 reveal beat is **the page that writes itself**:

- A page appears in the notebook *while the player is
  reading it*. Wet ink.
- The player can't pretend anymore.
- Page 11 (drafted) — the marginal voice interrupts mid-
  sentence and Dana acknowledges the interruption: *"I was
  going to write that. She's right. — D."*
- The signoff has shrunk from `— Dana` (page 8) to `— D.`

## Open questions

- COB is administratively complex but conceptually simple

  Does the Hydra fight do the concept justice, or does L9
  need a second encounter that isolates a specific COB rule?
- The Hydra's three heads talk over each other (per
  [`../../narrative/tone.md`](../../narrative/tone.md));
  worth scripting that dialogue carefully so the comedy
  lands.
