# Philosophy — two truths

What the cosmology *means*. The interpretive frame the player is
slowly learning to hold.

## The two truths

**The hospital is the truth of *people*.**

- Patient, doctor, family, registrar, biller.
- Names, faces, fatigue, kindness, mistakes.
- Cozy because human.

**The Waiting Room is the truth of *forms*.**

- CARC code, LCD criterion, NCCI edit, DRG, modifier 25.
- The system speaking in its own dialect.
- Surreal because it's the abstract made concrete.

Both real. Neither complete alone. The institution requires
both, can hold neither comfortably.

## How it gets said

Said aloud once or twice in the run. Once by Dana at L1 (sets
the philosophical frame for the whole game). Possibly echoed by
the player at L10 (marks their transformation).

Draft Dana line:

> *"There's the truth that bleeds, and there's the truth that
> bills. The hospital can't run without both. Most days you only
> have to hold one. Don't trust anyone who says they always know
> which is which."*

## The education the game is delivering

Most workers learn to hold one truth at a time. Clinicians live
in the people-truth and treat the form-truth as paperwork.
Administrators live in the form-truth and treat the people-truth
as out of scope. The player learns to hold *both*.

That's the actual education the game delivers. The revenue-cycle
content (CARC codes, LCDs, modifiers) is the vocabulary of the
form-truth. The hospital NPCs and their patients are the
texture of the people-truth. The player crosses between them on
purpose, level by level.

By L10, holding both should feel natural to the player — and
the audit boss is a stress test of whether they can defend
*both* (their work AND Dana's last claim) under pressure.
