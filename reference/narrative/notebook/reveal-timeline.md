# Reveal timeline — Dana's slow unraveling

How Dana's stuck-in-the-Waiting-Room state surfaces to the
player, level by level. The reveal *begins at L4* and builds
through L9 to the L10 audit.

The player doesn't get told. They notice.

## Per-level beats

### L1-L3 — fully present

Dana is calm, wise, fully present. Hands the player pages 1-3
of her notebook on day one. Says the philosophy line at L1.
Stays consistent through L2 and L3.

No cracks yet. The player has no reason to suspect anything.

### L4 — first cracks

**The level the player learns CDI / documentation.** The same
level where unresolved-documentation manifests as the Wraith
in the Waiting Room. The metaphor foreshadows itself.

Beats:
- Dana misses a meeting she should be at.
- Comes in late, smiles it off, says she "got lost downstairs."
- A page in the notebook now ends mid-sentence.
- A coffee cup on her desk has no coffee in it (steam-shaped
  emptiness).

The player isn't sure anything's wrong yet. It could be a bad
day. Most things on most days are bad days.

### L5 — ambient

- Dana sometimes refers to a meeting that hasn't happened.
  Doesn't seem to notice the slip.
- The handwriting on a recent page doesn't quite match her
  hand. Closer than a forgery; further than a draft.
- Around this level, she also gives the player a *blank*
  notebook (apprentice → peer; see
  [`README.md`](README.md)).

### L6 — first wet ink

- A page that was blank yesterday has writing on it today.
  The ink hasn't dried.
- The player can't catch her writing it. They never see her
  with the notebook open.

### L7 — the glimpse

- The player sees Dana in the Waiting Room — briefly,
  distantly. A figure at a desk that vanishes when looked at
  directly.
- When asked the next day, Dana doesn't remember.
- This is the moment the player *knows*. Doesn't have the
  vocabulary for it yet, but knows.

### L8 — the photo

- On Dana's desk in the hospital, there's a faded picture
  frame. Pediatric ward. The player notices it for the first
  time.
- Mentioned, not zoomed. (Foreshadowing only —
  Dana's last claim was for a child; see
  [`../characters/dana.md`](../characters/dana.md).)

### L9 — the page that writes itself

- A page appears in the notebook *while the player is reading
  it*. Wet ink.
- The player can't pretend anymore.

### L10 — the audit

The audit isn't just an audit — it's the door home. A claim
defended with full documentation can pull a trapped soul back
to the surface. Dana's last claim is in the file.

See [`../endings.md`](../endings.md) for the L10 reveal,
Dana's child case as the anchor, and the ending branches.

## Authoring rules

- **Build through small wrongness, not announcements.** The
  audience figures it out. We don't sit them down.
- **Dana is never spooky.** The cracks are *gentle*. She
  doesn't menace. She slips quietly. The horror is in how
  *normally* she presents.
- **The notebook is the canary.** When something's wrong with
  Dana, it shows up in the notebook first.
- **Wet ink is a one-time tool.** Use it once at L6 (subtly)
  and once at L9 (overtly). Don't overplay it.
