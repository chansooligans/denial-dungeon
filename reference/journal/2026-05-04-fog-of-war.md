# 2026-05-04 — Hospital fog of war + mini-map

## What changed
`HospitalScene` now restricts visibility to the room the player is currently
in. Previously the entire map was visible from spawn, which made the
multi-room hospital layouts feel less like a hospital and more like a
diagram.

## How it works

1. **Room detection** — At map load, flood-fill the layout. Walls (`W`),
   doors (`D`), and locked doors (`L`) are barriers; every other tile gets
   a `roomId`. Doors intentionally have `roomId = -1` so stepping onto a
   door tile does not switch the player into a new room mid-traversal.
2. **Three-state visibility** — Each tile carries one of:
   - `VIS_HIDDEN` (alpha 0): never seen
   - `VIS_VISITED` (alpha 0.28): seen, but not the player's current room
   - `VIS_CURRENT` (alpha 1): the room the player is standing in
3. **Room transitions** — When the player walks into a tile whose `roomId`
   differs from the current one, all `VIS_CURRENT` tiles demote to
   `VIS_VISITED`, then `revealRoom()` lights up the new room plus its
   bounding walls/doors (8-neighbor scan) so the room's shape stays
   readable.
4. **Entities follow** — NPCs and the crack hide / dim based on their
   tile's visibility state. NPC interaction prompts only fire when the
   NPC is in `VIS_CURRENT`, so you can't talk to someone you can't see.

## Mini-map
Top-right corner overlay (`scrollFactor = 0`, depth 99–101):
- Cell size auto-scales to keep the map under ~180 px wide.
- Walls render gray, doors yellow, crack purple, player teal.
- Visited rooms render at 0.45 alpha; current room at 1.0; unvisited not
  drawn at all.
- Repaints on room change; player dot re-renders every move.

## Trade-offs considered
- **Doors as barriers**: doors are flood-fill barriers but visually
  passable. Without this, every map collapsed into one connected region
  (lobby, corridors, and rooms all share open floor through doorways)
  and "current room" became meaningless.
- **Outdoor margins**: most maps have 1–2 tiles of "outdoor" floor between
  the building wall and the map border. These flood-fill into their own
  region. They're unreachable in gameplay (no door connects them), so
  they stay permanently `VIS_HIDDEN` — which is fine.
- **Door tiles unassigned**: a door belongs to neither adjacent room.
  `revealRoom` paints adjacent walls/doors when revealing a room, so
  doors of the current room appear at full alpha; doors between two
  visited-but-not-current rooms render at the higher of the two states.
